Sandra Omilo

Sandra is a graduate of Babcock University, an inspirational lifestyle blogger and a Brand Strategy enthusiast, who enjoys making positive impacts wherever she finds herself. She is passionate about personal growth and development and is driven by the values of self-love and determination. She would like to solve the problem of copyright infringement in her industry.

When she is not meeting deadlines at work, Sandra is blogging and watching movies. Her three favourite podcasts/books are “Bants Books and Broke”, “Leadership” by Brian Stacy and “As a Man Thinketh” by James Allen. She likes them because they’re engaging, relatable and teach the importance of thinking positively to achieve one's life desires, respectively.

FAVOURITE QUOTE

“Don’t stop when you are tired, stop when you are done.”

PROUDEST ACHIEVEMENT

Catering for the welfare of the students in my hostel as a senator of the hall in Babcock University Student Association (BUSA), 2018/19 session.

IF YOU WERE A BRAND, WHICH WOULD YOU BE AND WHY?

Nike - This brand is inspiring and it reflects my core values.

WHAT DO YOU LIKE ABOUT WORKING AT ANAKLE?

I love the fact that the environment is so flexible yet, we come up with amazing ideas!

Henry Opene

Henry is passionate about art and creativity, which is evidenced in his involvement in hand-painted art —something he has been doing for a while. Recently, he migrated to using digital graphics to create his art. He is driven by the values of learning and growth. This is why he never shies away from learning more when an opportunity presents itself.

One problem he would like to solve for his industry is helping entrepreneurs. When Henry isn’t working, he is watching creative Youtube videos. His top three favourite books are “Harry Potter” by J.K. Rowling; “Rich Dad, Poor Dad” by Robert Kiyosaki; and “Star Trek” by David Alan. He likes them because they're inspiring and adventurous.

FAVOURITE QUOTE

IF YOU WERE A BRAND, WHICH WOULD YOU BE AND WHY?

WHAT DO YOU LIKE ABOUT WORKING AT ANAKLE?

I love the working structure at Anakle.

Michelle Efuntoye-Dave

Michelle has a degree in Primary Education and grew up reading books bought by her mom. She plays the cello and is passionate about people management and books. She is driven by the values of hard work, tenacity, love, and personal growth. When she is not working, she enjoys reading, binge-watching series and writing in her book blog. In her industry, she would like to change the stereotype that people managers are unapproachable. Her three favourite podcasts/books are “The Young God” podcast, “Factfulness” by Hans Rosling and “Who Will Cry When You Die” by Robin Sharma. She likes them because they’re really big on self-reliance and growth.

FAVOURITE QUOTE

PROUDEST ACHIEVEMENT

IF YOU WERE A BRAND, WHICH WOULD YOU BE AND WHY?

WHAT DO YOU LIKE ABOUT WORKING AT ANAKLE?

Working with many young professionals.

Unwana Umosen

Unwana is a Mass Communication undergraduate of Covenant University. She had dreams of being a musician but that changed as she grew older and found love in English. She is passionate about all kinds of art and enjoys working at Anakle for a lot of reasons but most especially the exposure to a team of dedicated people. An industry problem that bothers her is how poorly brands are represented as a result of bad quality of content. When she isn’t working, she is creating a fun moment for herself, with or without people. One of the most creative books she has read is “Elsewhere” by Gabrielle Zevin which talks about love and death. Other books she loves are “Faceless” by Amma Darko and “You” by Max Lucado.

FAVOURITE QUOTE

No hype but he says, “the opportunity to learn a new thing every day and test myself across various teams” makes him enjoy being at Anakle.

He considers “Shoe Dog” by Phil Knight; “The Grand Weaver” by Ravi Zacharias; and “Almost Everything” by Jeffrey Archer as his top three books because they are a blend of the most amazing stories, life lessons and spiritual guidance. When Solomon is not working, you can catch him reading books and blogs or maybe watching a movie.

Julius Omunizua

Julius holds a Mass Communication degree from Babcock University. During his university days, he served in several leadership positions including Director of Public Relations for Babcock University Students Association 2018/2019 (BUSA) and P.R.O for Mass Communication Students Association 2017/2018. A strong value that drives him is diligence. His three favourite books are “Giovanni's Room” by James Baldwin, “Americanah” by Chimamanda Ngozi Adichie and “Gone Girl” by Gillian Flynn. He is passionate about marketing, human rights, public speaking and film production. Julius owns a bespoke tailoring line called 'JOMUNI' and he is a freelance voiceover artist. When Julius is not working, he is busy dancing in front of the mirror or singing in the shower.

Ruth Oye

Ruth is an expressive person even though she is an introvert. A graduate of International Relations, she has a lot of ideas that she would love to bring to reality. She enjoys talking and being able to provide solutions. She believes that the digital marketing industry is saturated with a lot of people claiming what they are not. If given the opportunity, she would advise digital marketers to specialise in one key area of digital marketing. Empathy, integrity and honesty are some of the values that drive her.

Her top three favourite books are “Think Big” by Ben Carson, “Gifted Hands” by Ben Carson and “The Temperament” by Laraine Bennett. When she is not working, she enjoys reading novels, seeing movies at the cinemas and being in her own space alone as it gives her time to reflect. She is currently doing her Master’s in Diplomacy and Strategic Studies and considering building a career in brand communications.

FAVOURITE QUOTE

“You only live once. But if you do it right, once is enough.”

IF YOU WERE A BRAND, WHICH WOULD YOU BE AND WHY?

Nike. The brand is young, simple and they are full of innovations at every step. "Just do it" is the mantra we share.

What are you passionate about?
